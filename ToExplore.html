Jewel Changi Airport

Discover Jewel Changi Airport!  a top-notch entertainment and shopping complex with a natural theme that is connected to and linked to Changi Airport in Singapore. Its most iconic symbol is the Rain Vortex, which is also the world's tallest indoor waterfall. Not only is it a tourist attraction, but there are also lots of activities for all ages.
Fun Fact: It is also one of the largest indoor Garden in Singapore

Some of the activities that you can do there are the Walking Net,Mirror Maze and the Hedge Maze. Theres so much more that you can do there, such as visiting Pokemon Centre or chill at the Lounge


Damage $-$$$
Operating Hours 24 hours
Address 78 Airport Blvd., Singapore 819666
Connected to the Arrival Hall (level 1) of Terminal 1.









Garden By The Bay

Visit Singapore's greatest botanical site, a must-see tourist hotspot. The Garden By The Bay. An award-winning super garden, created at a cost of over a billion dollars, includes both indoor and outdoor gardens that fully immerse you in the world of plants. It covers over 250 acres in Singapore's central region and has more than 2,200 different plant species. Additionally, it received the title of Largest Glass Greenhouse (Flower Dome) in the 2015 Guinness World Records.



Budget $-$$
Attractions: Cloud Forest
SuperTree Grove
Floral Fantasy
Flower Dome






Sentosa

VVisit Sentosa, a famous and massive resort island in Singapore. It is packed with a ton of enjoyable activities for you and your loved ones. Most of Singapore's tropical beaches are located in Sentosa. A free bus service that circles the island and a free monorail service makes it simple to reach all of the major attractions. Be it having fun at USS or suntanning at the beach, it will definitely be a day well spent.

Chinatown

You definitely must go to Chinatown when you are in Singapore. Chinatown is well-known for its stunning fusion of old and modern, featuring numerous historic temples and bustling streets. You can enjoy a variety of things to do here, such as visiting temples, shopping for souvenirs at Chinatown Street Markets, and enjoying the Chinatown Murals, paintings on old buildings portraying scenes from the old days.



Budget $
Things to do Visit Buddha Tooth Relic Temple
Visit Sri Mariamman Temple
Shopping at Chinatown Street Market
Take pictures while learning about the Chinatown Murals
Learning Chinese Culture at Chinatown Heritage Center
